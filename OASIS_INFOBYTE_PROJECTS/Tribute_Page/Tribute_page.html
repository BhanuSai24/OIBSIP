Ratan Tata

Indian industrialist and former chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group.

About the Legend

☛ Ratan Naval Tata, born on December 28, 1937, is an Indian industrialist and philanthropist.

☛ He served as the chairman of Tata Sons for over two decades, leading the Tata Group, one of India's largest and oldest conglomerates.

☛ Ratan Tata is known for transforming the Tata Group into a global enterprise with interests in diverse sectors such as steel, automobiles, telecommunications, and hospitality.

☛ Under his leadership, Tata Motors launched the Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car, in 2008.

☛ Ratan Tata has been a prominent figure in the Indian business world and has received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to industry and society.

☛ In recognition of his achievements, he was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 2000 and the Padma Vibhushan in 2008, two of India's highest civilian honors.
